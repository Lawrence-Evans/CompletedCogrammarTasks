John Wyndham

Science fiction writer

"Intelligent life is the only thing that gives meaning to the universe. It is a holy thing, to be fostered and treasured. Without it nothing begins, nothing ends, there can be nothing through all eternity but the mindless babblings of chaos . . . Therefore, the nurture of all intelligent forms is a sacred duty. Even the merest spark of reason must be fanned in the hope of a flame. Frustrated intelligence must have its bonds broken. Narrow-channelled intelligence must be given the power to widen out. High intelligence must be learned from." - Chocky 1968

Timeline

  • Born 10th July 1903 in Dorridge, Warwickshire, England.
  • When he was 8 his parents separated. He and his brothe became estraged from their father and remained with his mother in Edgbaston.
  • He received a private education in a series of boarding school. He left Bedales School, Hampshire in 1921 aged 18.
  • Wyndham worked in serveral careers: farming, law, commercial art and advertising.
  • His writing career began in 1925 and his first published work was The Curse of the Burdens, as by John Beynon Harris in 1927 a detective novel.
  • He lived in London, where his politics and viewa were influuncd by a mixture of pacifists, socialists and communists. Informing his opionins on social engineering and feminism.
  • Met his future wife, Grace Wilson, a teacher. They embarked on a long-lasting love affair while he lived in London.
  • During the Second World War he worked as a censor for the Ministory of Information and was a firewatcher in the Home Guard during the Blitz.
  • He joined the Army and served as cipher operator in the Royal Corps of Signals. During this time he would write to his partner Grace about his struggles with his conscience, doubts about humanity and fears of the war continuing. Also his love for her and fear that he would be so tainted by war that she would not be able to love him when he returned.
  • Until 1951 he wrote sciecnce fiction novels and short stories under his pen name.
  • Changing his writing style he wrote the novel The Day of the Triffids,using the John Wyndham pen name for the first time. It was extremely successful.
  • He would go on to write many novels and was considered a visionary of the science fiction genre.
  • In 1963 he married Grace Wilson having known each other for 30 years. They lived outside Petersfield, Hampshire and were childless.
  • He died in 1969 aged 65. His work contines to inspire writers, artists and filmakers.
